How will the universe end?
I believe that over time as stars start to die out and are sucked into black holes that they will be
destroyed. When this happens the atoms that are being sucked into the black hole sometime are
not fully sucked in and this can be achieved when the atoms are split. For example, an electron
could get sucked in but the protons can be shot out appearing as radiation. This will allow for the
black holes to disperse energy until the universe has spread so far apart that this radiation is
undetectable. Over time this loss of energy will cause blackholes to evaporate. Civilization will have
to live off of the rotational energy of blackholes. The main heart of the theory is not just how the
universe will be destroyed but how it had formed. I believe that these were the same processes.
Since there are multiple universes, some will have the right physics and characteristics to allow
intelligent life to form. These civilizations were smart enough to live trillions and trillions of years but  as matter starts to theoretically disintegrate these advances civilization will try to start a new 
universe. It would also require a seed particle called a ‘monopole’ (which is hypothesised to exist by
some models of physics, but has yet to be found). The idea goes that if we could impart enough
energy to a monopole, it will start to inflate. Rather than growing in size within our Universe, the
expanding monopole would bend spacetime within the accelerator to create a tiny wormhole tunnel
leading to a separate region of space. From within our lab we would see only the mouth of the
wormhole; it would appear to us as a mini black hole, so small as to be utterly harmless. But if we
could travel into that wormhole, we would pass through a gateway into a rapidly expanding baby
universe that had been artificially created. This makes me believe that this is how our universe
formed and in is our duty as a civilization to create an artificial universe.

- Cole Frazier
